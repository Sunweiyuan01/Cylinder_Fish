The fishmove folder contains the CFD case files and simulation resources for the fish–cylinder interaction problem. The case is designed so that the cylinder wake is allowed to develop into a stable vortical state before the fish begins active locomotion. For this reason, the simulation at the beginning of each run typically loads a previously saved flow-field state, rather than starting from a completely undeveloped initial condition. This treatment avoids unnecessary startup transients and ensures that the fish interacts with a physically meaningful and repeatable wake environment from the early stage of the simulation. In terms of motion implementation, the case combines dynamic mesh deformation with overset mesh techniques. The body undulation of the fish is imposed through dynamic mesh updating, while the global translation and maneuvering of the swimmer are handled through the overset mesh framework. This hybrid strategy makes it possible to capture both large-scale body motion and local shape deformation in a stable and computationally efficient manner, thereby providing a suitable CFD foundation for studying wake interaction, obstacle avoidance, and flow-aware locomotion control.
